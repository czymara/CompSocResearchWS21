3. Random intercept(s) models

Now we are ready to run some multi-level models. Compared to the last sessions, these models will have random intercepts on several levels: individuals (level 1) are nested in NUTS2 regions (level 2), NUTS2 regions are nested in NUTS1 regions (level 3), and NUTS1 regions are nested in countries (level 4). We skip the NUTS3 level because the five level model will not converge and due to the representativity issues raised above.

a) Null model

To understand the correct random effects structure, we start with the null model. Use lmer() to estimate a null model that has the following random effects structure, where \(plinsoc_{ijkl}\) is the perceived place in society of individual \(i\) living in NUTS2 region \(j\) within NUTS1 region \(k\) of country \(l\). \(u_{0l}\) is the random intercept on the country-level (between countries), \(u_{0kl}\) the random intercept on the NUTS1 level (within countries), , \(u_{0jkl}\) the random intercept on the NUTS2 level (within NUTS1), and \(e_{ijkl}\) is the individual level error term (within NUTS2):

\[plinsoc_{ijkl}=\beta_{0}+u_{0l}+u_{0kl}+u_{0jkl}+e_{ijkl}\]

Then calculate the ICC to obtain the share of variance of plinsoc on each level (either manually or use the performance package).

# models

4. Random slope of education

Finally, letâs test whether the relationship between years of education and perceived place in society differs across contexts. As we now have four levels, we could let the individual-level education effect vary on each of the three higher levels.

a) Between countries

Model a random slope of eduyrs between countries (other variables not shown in equation for simplicity):

\[plinsoc_{ijkl}=\beta_0+\beta_1eduyrs_{ijkl}+u_{0l}+\boldsymbol{u_{1l}}*eduyrs_{ijkl}+u_{0kl}+u_{0jkl}+e_{ijkl}\]

m3_RS_c <- lmer(plinsoc ~ c_gdp_eurhab_2011 + n1_gdp_eurhab_2011 + n2_gdp_eurhab_2011 + 
                        factor(hincfel) + eduyrs + gndr + (1 + eduyrs | cntry) + (1 | cntry:NUTS1) + (1 | cntry:NUTS1:NUTS2),
              data = ESS6_NUTS123)

b) Between NUTS1 regions

Model a random slope of eduyrs between NUTS1 regions (within countries):

\[plinsoc_{ijkl}=\beta_0+\beta_1eduyrs_{ijkl}+u_{0l}+u_{0kl}+\boldsymbol{u_{1kl}}*eduyrs_{ijkl}+u_{0jkl}+e_{ijkl}\]

Compare to the results of 4.a).
